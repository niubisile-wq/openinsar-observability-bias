# Manuscript Outline v1
Date: 2026-07-09

## One-sentence argument
In open InSAR monitoring of land subsidence, we show that public-product observability is structured censoring rather than random missingness, and that this censoring systematically underestimates exposure, using area-weighted Chao Phraya evidence, cross-product transfer to Japan and Iran, and robustness controls, with the boundary that Europe-scale dense benchmark closure still requires EGMS credentials.
